Case Study

Situation

During the COVID, the client archived the web project my team worked in for years as a frontend team leader. There were massive layoffs and only a couple of the team including me were transferred to the main application team.

Task

The task or challenge was to become a proficient backend developer in C# in time record, since according to my skills it should not be (apparently) too hard.

Action

As soon I was assigned with this task, I requested help from the base company to have a mentor in this technology, who I can create an schedule, milestones and a deadline with.

Support

Many colleagues tried to support me with tons of documentation, but I knew the metacognition leaning roadmap starts from teasing our brains with the practical (creating the challenge by doing), and understanding the principles by teaching.

Mentoring

The exchange experience with this very professional mentor, enabled the heuristics by:

  • Finding similarities in backend and frontend languages
  • Enriching my background on design and architecture patterns
  • Refining the methods employed to breakdown a task understanding the client need (time vs quality)
  • Enhancing the argumenting skills at explaining the duration of a task

Result

Although the initial estimate was overlapped by a couple of weeks, the resulting skill set obtained was long-lasting.

As achievement we not only reduced the dependency in other peers to finish the features, but also, brought value by adding features such as:

  • Unit testing coverage
  • Lambda function
  • Investment of control

Among other approaches to the current technology base, in order to simplify our code (increase readability and reduce maintenance) and reduce the technical debt.
